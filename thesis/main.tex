\begin{abstract}
This thesis studies realistic resource requirements for applying Grover's algorithm to symmetric cryptanalysis under fault-tolerant assumptions.
The work investigates how error-correcting architectures impact feasibility, with emphasis on quantum memory and logical operations.
Implementations and simulations are developed for small code families, including the 5-qubit and 7-qubit codes, and exploratory analysis is performed for LDPC-style constructions such as surface and BB codes.
The results provide practical insight into which design assumptions dominate runtime and overhead in near-term-to-fault-tolerant pathways.
\end{abstract}
